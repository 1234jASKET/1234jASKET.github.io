France: Emmanuel Macron denounces a "manhunt" against Gérard Depardieu, accused of rape and sexual assault by a young actress. The French president affirms that the actor is "innocent until proven otherwise" and that we must not "condemn without judgment"

Quebec: A 51-year-old woman, Céline Dion's niece, lost her life in a road accident yesterday in Sainte-Anne-des-Plaines. His 16-year-old daughter, who was driving, was seriously injured. The singer's family expressed their sadness and support

International: Tension is rising between Ukraine and Russia, which accuses Kiev of preparing a military offensive in Donbass, a pro-Russian separatist region. Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky called for a meeting with his Russian counterpart Vladimir Putin to avoid an escalation of the conflict.

Culture: British actor Tom Wilkinson, known for his roles in films such as The Full Monty, Shakespeare in Love or Batman Begins, died at the age of 72. He had been suffering from lung cancer for several months.

Sport: The French women's football team won the SheBelieves Cup, a friendly tournament organized in the United States, by beating Brazil 2-1 in the final. Les Bleues scored their third consecutive victory in the competition, after dominating England and the United States
