Jesus Christ according to Saint John (John 13:31-33a, 34-35) At that time, After Judas had left, Jesus said: “Now the Son of Man is glorified, and God is glorified in him. If God is glorified in him, God will also glorify him in himself, and he will glorify him at once. My little children, I am with you only a little longer. I give you a new commandment: Love one another. As I have loved you, so you also should love one another. By this, everyone will know that you are my disciples, if you have love for one another.

Meditation John 13:31-35 In this passage, Jesus delivers one of his most intimate and profound teachings to his disciples. Judas has just left to betray him, and Jesus, knowing that his hour has come, leaves them with a final commandment: Love one another as I have loved you. 1. Glory in the Cross: Jesus speaks of his glorification through the Cross. For the world, the Cross is a symbol of weakness, but for Jesus, it is the pinnacle of sacrificial love. It is here that the true glory of God is revealed — not in power or domination, but in self-giving love to the very end. 2. A New Commandment: The commandment to love is not new in itself, but what is new is the way Jesus calls us to love: “As I have loved you.” • It is a love that forgives, even those who betray us. • A love that serves, as Jesus did when he washed his disciples’ feet. • A love willing to give one’s life, even for one’s enemies. 3. A Distinctive Sign: Jesus teaches us that fraternal love is the distinctive sign of his disciples. It is not through great works or miracles that the world will recognize his followers but through their love for one another. This love must be visible, concrete, and embodied. Practical Application: • Today, let us ask ourselves: How do I love? • Is it a conditional, self-serving love, or a love that gives freely? • Is there someone I find difficult to love? How can I take a step toward them today, in the image of Jesus?

Lecture du livre des Actes des Apôtres (14, 21b-27) En ces jours-là, Paul et Barnabé retournèrent à Lystres, à Iconium et à Antioche de Pisidie. Ils affermissaient le courage des disciples ; ils les exhortaient à persévérer dans la foi, en disant : « Il nous faut passer par bien des épreuves pour entrer dans le royaume de Dieu. » Ils désignèrent des anciens pour chacune de leurs Églises, après avoir prié et jeûné ; ils les confièrent au Seigneur, en qui ils avaient mis leur foi.

Lecture de l’Apocalypse de saint Jean (21, 1-5a) Moi, Jean, j’ai vu un ciel nouveau et une terre nouvelle. Car le premier ciel et la première terre avaient disparu, et il n’y avait plus de mer. Et j’ai vu descendre du ciel, d’auprès de Dieu, la ville sainte, la Jérusalem nouvelle, préparée comme une épouse qui s’est parée pour son époux. Et j’entendis une voix forte, qui venait du Trône ; elle disait : « Voici la demeure de Dieu avec les hommes ; il habitera avec eux, ils seront son peuple, et lui-même, Dieu avec eux, sera leur Dieu.
